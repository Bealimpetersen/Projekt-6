Why choose QuadSAT?


QuadSAT adds a new dimension to antenna diagnostics. The compact system is transported directly to the antenna and autonomously flies in a pre-planned pattern, resulting in a high quality, operationally flexible, and time-saving solution. QuadSAT’s unique solution enables a range of on-site measurements such as antenna radiation patterns and antenna alignment. The payload mounted on the drone enables maximal location flexibility. As a mobile test-facility, it can operate anywhere in the world on antennas of all sizes.

Drone

QuadSAT´s unique solution is an airborne antenna and tracking test system. With the QuadSAT system, antennas can be tested anytime and anywhere. The system can perform a broad range of testing missions to suit your needs, whether out in the field for operational antennas or in the testing phase for new antennas.QuadSAT's system turns a drone into a flying antenna diagnostics system. The system is easy to use and portable enabling it to be used where needed. With a QuadSAT system, tests are performed where and when needed reducing operational expenditure

Testing


Pre-testing: QuadSAT’s technician arrives directly on site of the antenna. Mission characteristics as antenna size and frequency are entered in QuadSAT’s custom pre-flight software prior to testing.


Test: Based on QuadSAT’s pre-flight software, the drone-based test range lifts off and automatically performs the antenna measurements. The tested antenna remains in it’s ordinary operation. The automation provided in QuadSAT’s solution ensures repeatable and accurate results with every flight.


Post testing: QuadSAT’s post-flight software delivers a detailed report based on customer requirements. All tests results are delivered in the same uniform data format that ensures comparability of test-data and makes it possible to follow an antennas performance over time.

The software

The system includes custom pre- and post-flight software for automated flight and antenna diagnostics. QuadSAT software automatically generates a report on test results. 2D and 3D diagrams for patterns for cuts and raster scans combined with raw data

How it works:
- Drone-based system transmits CW test signal to antenna under test (AUT) which is in Rx mode
- AUT receive and transmit patterns are generated using reciprocity principle
- Signal received by antenna is measured with spectrum analyser and power meter connected to the ground control station
- RTK base station provides increased accuracy of drone GPS position (3 cm)
- The flight of the drone is planned and automated from the ground control station
- QuadSAT’s unique solution enables a range of on-site measurements e.g. antenna radiation patterns and antenna alignment. The payload mounted on the drone enables maximal location flexibility. As a mobile test-facility, it can operate anywhere in the world on antennas of all sizes.
